Guide 4. How to print your 3D model.

This guide is useful for everyone regardless of their 3D printing knowledge and experience.
If you want to make a real object from a 3D computer model, you might have the following options: 
  • Get a 3D printer at home
  • Find a neighbor that has a 3D printer or
  • Use a consumer or professional service.
Even if you don't have your own 3D printer, you have to understand these basic concepts before going further.

Important 3D printing concepts

  1. The 3D object has to be made in certain material and color
    • The material will define properties of the end object: color, durability, price, finish etc
    • Some materials will allow you to choose color or even make multicolor objects
  2. Chosen material will limit technology, brand and model of 3D printer to be used
  3. 3D printers are very different and they have unique limitations, pros and cons, for example:
    • Maximum size of the object that can be printed in one piece
    • Possibility to print multi-material or multi-color objects
    • Available materials and/or material combinations
    • Accuracy
    • Time and cost of printing
  4. Your 3D model has to have certain properties, that may differ depending on chosen technology or model of the 3D printer, for example:
    • 3D model has to fit in the 3D printer's "build chamber"
    • It has to be watertight (have no holes)
    • Surface normals should be oriented correctly
    • Preferably, there should be no hollow areas and self-intersections
  5. 3D Printers build objects layer by layer and the object orientation matters:
    • Printer accuracy is different in Z and X/Y dimensions. Most printers build parts bottom to top and you may see printing layers on the end object
    • Thin walls and small details may not be printed or may result in damaged part
    • End objects might have different strength in each of the X, Y and Z directions
  6. It is possible to add finishing style to your object e.g polish and paint or assemble multiple objects together.
3DView allows detecting and fixing these common issues. These guiles might help you to detect issues and repair your model.
If it is too complex for to you - consider consumer service that might help you with your project.

Getting a 3D printer

Desktop 3D Printers became much more affordable in resent years. 3D printer at home can be quite useful and is fun for the whole family.



Please note that most desktop printers use FDM or similar technologies with their strengths and limitations. They make made quite durable objects; offer relatively cheap ABS or PLA plastic materials but are very slow and have low accuracy.
First and most known home 3D printer is Makerbot. Nowadays there are many other home 3D printers on the market. You may also check this and this articles that compares home 3D printers side by side.
